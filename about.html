HISTORY

Roseville State College emerged in 1990 as the Roseville Community Institute, born from a local desire for greater educational opportunities and economic growth. Starting with practical vocational training, the institute quickly gained community support and expanded its offerings. By 2002, recognizing its growth and broader academic focus, it officially became Roseville State College. This transition marked an era of new programs in vital fields like Information Technology, Engineering, and Nursing, alongside significant investments in modern learning facilities. Throughout its history, the college has maintained a strong commitment to accessibility, ensuring education is within reach for local residents. It has also actively fostered partnerships with community organizations and businesses, providing students with real-world experiences and contributing to the region's development. Today, Roseville State College stands as a valued institution, dedicated to academic excellence, innovation in teaching, and empowering students to become successful and engaged members of their community, honoring the foundational vision of its early leaders.
